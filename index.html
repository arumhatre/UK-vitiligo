WHAT IS VITILIGO?

Vitiligo, also commonly known as Leucoderma, is a common skin pigmentation disorder characterized by irregular, pale or milky white patches on the body. Vitiligo causes can be due to several reasons; however, this happens when melanocytes, the pigment which gives our skin its colour, start malfunctioning resulting in white patches or pigmentations.

causes of vitiligo

Where Does Vitiligo Show Up on the Body?

A person's skin can develop vitiligo anywhere on their body. When vitiligo first develops, the patches typically show up on the:

Face

Arms

Hands

Feet

Over time, the vitiligo may spread to other parts of a person's body as the spots and patches enlarge. Some persons have a color loss in mucous membranes, which include the genitalia and the inside of the mouth and nose.Additionally, vitiligo causes white or prematurely grey hair on the scalp. Some patients have some color loss in their eyes and notice luminous spots on the colored portion of their eyes.

Types of Vitiligo

The different types of vitiligo skin disease are as follows:

Vitiligo Vulgaris
Focal Vitiligo
Segmental Vitiligo
Acrofacial Vulgaris
Vitiligo Universalis

Nonsegmental Vitiligo:

The most common form of vitiligo, characterized by pale skin patches that usually affect both sides of the body. The earliest symptoms may appear on the feet, hands, wrists, or areas around the eyes or mouth. Another name for non-segmental vitiligo is bilateral or generalized vitiligo.

Segmental Vitiligo:

White spots frequently only show up on one side of the body, such as one arm or leg rather than both. It's typical for hair color to fade. Segmented vitiligo can develop at a young age. It could advance quickly for six months to two years, then cease. This type of vitiligo may reactivate years later in unusual circumstances.

Symptoms of vitiligo

Based on the type of vitiligo disease, the symptoms of vitiligo can be as listed below:

  • Loss of skin color in patches or spots. White spots on the skin usually first appear on the hands (white spots on hands), face (vitiligo on face), mouth (vitiligo on lips), and genitals.
  • Loss of color in the tissues that line the inside of your nose and mouth (mucous membranes).
  • Premature greying or whitening of the hair on your scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, or beard.

Vitiligo's Social And Psychological Impacts

The condition of vitiligo is neither contagious nor life-threatening. However, those who have vitiligo disease may slip into social isolation and face great emotional stress because of the noticeable white patches on their skin. In extreme circumstances, it may cause despair and suicidal thoughts in people.
